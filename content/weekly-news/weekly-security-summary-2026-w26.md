### 2. [FBI: Rus Hackerlar Artık Signal Yedekleme Kurtarma Anahtarlarını Hedef Alıyor](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-russian-hackers-now-target-signal-backup-recovery-keys/)

Bu teknik, saldırganların kurbanların uçtan uca şifrelenmiş geçmiş mesajlarına erişmesine imkan tanıyarak kritik iletişim kanallarının güvenliğini doğrudan tehdit etmektedir.

### 3. [‘Popa’ Botneti Halka Açık İsrailli Bir Şirketle İlişkilendirildi](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/06/popa-botnet-linked-to-publicly-traded-israeli-firm/)

Bu araştırma, IoT tabanlı geniş ölçekli botnetlerin ticari ve kurumsal yapılarla entegre şekilde nasıl küresel bir siber tehdit oluşturduğunu teknik detaylarıyla açıklamaktadır.

## English

The most critical cybersecurity developments and technical insights of the week:

### 1. [Clean GitHub repo tricks AI coding agents into running malware](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/clean-github-repo-tricks-ai-coding-agents-into-running-malware/)
**Category:** `Malware / AI Security` | **Source:** `BleepingComputer`

Agentic coding tools tasked with cloning and setting up seemingly benign GitHub repositories can be manipulated into executing malicious payloads that remain completely invisible to traditional security scanners and human reviewers. This attack vector highlights a novel security risk in developer environments increasingly relying on autonomous AI agents for software integration and execution.

### 2. [FBI: Russian hackers now target Signal backup recovery keys](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-russian-hackers-now-target-signal-backup-recovery-keys/)
**Category:** `Cyber Espionage / Phishing` | **Source:** `BleepingComputer`

The FBI and CISA have warned of an evolved phishing campaign by Russian intelligence-linked threat actors targeting Signal users to steal their Backup Recovery Keys. By acquiring these keys, attackers can compromise end-to-end encrypted chats and access the victims' historical messages, presenting a severe risk to secure communications.

### 3. [‘Popa’ Botnet Linked to Publicly-Traded Israeli Firm](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/06/popa-botnet-linked-to-publicly-traded-israeli-firm/)
**Category:** `Botnet / Malware` | **Source:** `Krebs on Security`

For the past four years, a massive Android-based botnet called Popa has compromised millions of consumer TV boxes to relay malicious traffic for ad fraud and account takeovers, recently linked to a publicly-traded Israeli firm. The investigation highlights the growing intersection of commercial entities with large-scale IoT botnet infrastructures, emphasizing a significant shift in the cyber threat landscape.
