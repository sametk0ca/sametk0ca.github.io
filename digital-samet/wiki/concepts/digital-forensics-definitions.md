# Digital Forensics Definitions

- **Source:** [[ka-forensics]]
- **Tags:** #forensics #definitions

## Overview
Digital forensics, also known as digital forensic science, is the process of identifying and reconstructing the relevant sequence of events that led to the currently observable state of a target IT system or digital artifacts.

## Key Definitions

### DFRWS Definition (2001)
> Digital forensics is the use of scientifically derived and proven methods toward the preservation, collection, validation, identification, analysis, interpretation, documentation and presentation of digital evidence derived from digital sources for the purpose of facilitating or furthering the reconstruction of events found to be criminal, or helping to anticipate unauthorized actions shown to be disruptive to planned operations. [[ka-forensics]]

### NIST Definition (SP 800-86)
Focuses on the legal aspects and chain of custody:
> The application of science to the identification, collection, examination, and analysis of data while preserving the integrity of the information and maintaining a strict chain of custody for the data. [[ka-forensics]]

### Working Definition
> The process of identifying and reconstructing the relevant sequence of events that led to the currently observable state of a target IT system or digital artifacts. [[ka-forensics]]

## Perspectives
- **Legal Perspective:** Focuses on admissibility, chain of custody, and proof in court.
- **Technical Perspective:** Focuses on reconstructing past events and understanding system behavior.
- **Operational Perspective:** Includes incident response and cyber defense, often for anticipating attacks rather than just prosecution.
