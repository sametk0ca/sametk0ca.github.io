# Concept: Human Error and Rule-Bending

## Overview
Virtually all human mistakes are predictable and occur due to a combination of **latent failures** (organizational/workplace conditions) and **active failures** (errors by individuals).

## The Swiss Cheese Model
Adapted from James Reason, this model suggests that a security incident occurs when a threat passes through "holes" (vulnerabilities) in successive layers of defense.
- **Active Failure**: A person clicking a malicious link or pushing the wrong button.
- **Latent Failure**: Underlying issues like poor system design, high workload, or conflicting organizational goals that set the stage for the active failure.

## Rule-Bending for Productivity
Most security non-compliance is actually **"rule-bending"**—a choice between following security and maintaining productivity.
- **Shadow Security**: Employees often create their own "amateur" security solutions that are workable for them, even if not fully secure.
- **Grudge Sale**: Security is often viewed as a "grudge" because it doesn't immediately contribute to the bottom line and often disrupts primary tasks.

## Thinking Fast and Slow (Kahneman)
- **System 1 (Fast)**: Automatic, routine, and efficient. 90% of human activity. Security checks here are often skipped (e.g., forgetting to check for a padlock icon).
- **System 2 (Slow)**: Conscious, step-by-step, and effortful. Used for new or complex tasks. 
- **The "Stop and Think" Fallacy**: Telling users to "stop and think" for every action (like clicking an email link) is unrealistic and disruptive. If the primary task cannot be completed without the risky action, System 1 will likely prevail.

## Types of Error
- **Slips and Lapses**: Failures in recognition, memory, or attention (Automatic mode).
- **Mistakes (Type I)**: Choosing the incorrect response despite thinking about it (Mixed mode).
- **Mistakes (Type II)**: Not knowing the correct response at all (Conscious mode).

## Recommendations
- **Security Hygiene**: Never issue a policy that cannot be followed.
- **Investigate Near-Misses**: Treat non-compliance as a symptom of latent failure to be fixed, not just a person to be punished.
- **DMARC and Technical Defenses**: Use technology to reduce the number of conscious security decisions a user must make.
