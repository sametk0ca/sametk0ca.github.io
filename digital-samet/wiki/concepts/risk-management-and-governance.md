# Concept: Risk Assessment and Management Principles

## Component vs. Systems Perspectives
According to UK NCSC guidance, risk management is divided into two main approaches:

### 1. Component-driven (Bottom-up)
- **Focus**: Individual technical components (hardware, software, data, staff).
- **Best for**: Less complex systems, well-understood connections, agreed physical functions.
- **Audience**: Operational employees.

### 2. System-driven (Top-down)
- **Focus**: Analyzing the system as a whole, its goals, and interdependencies.
- **Best for**: Complex interactions, establishing requirements before physical design, multi-stakeholder views.
- **Audience**: Higher-level managers and strategic decision-makers.

## Core Elements of Risk
Regardless of the method used, four concepts are central to risk assessment:

- **[[vulnerability|Vulnerability]]**: Something open to attack or misuse (socio-technical: technology, people, legal).
- **[[threat|Threat]]**: Individual, event, or action capable of exploiting a vulnerability (e.g., hackers, poorly designed processes).
- **[[likelihood|Likelihood]]**: Degree of possibility (qualitative or quantitative) that a threat will exploit a vulnerability.
- **[[impact|Impact]]**: Negative effect on system objectives if a threat is realized.
