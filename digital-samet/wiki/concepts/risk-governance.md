# Concept: Risk Governance

## Definition
Risk governance is an overarching set of ongoing processes and principles that aim to ensure awareness and education of the risks faced, and to instil a sense of responsibility and accountability to all involved in managing it.

## Governance Models
- **Technocratic**: Policy directly informed by science and evidence from domain expertise.
- **Decisionistic**: Risk evaluation and policy developed using inputs beyond science alone (e.g., social and economic drivers).
- **Transparent (Inclusive)**: Risk assessment context considered from the outset with input from science, politics, economics, and civil society.

## Successful Governance Elements
- **Coupled with Everyday Activity**: Cyber risk as important as health and safety, financial processes, etc.
- **Cultural Adoption**: Security must be part of everyday operational culture.
- **Human Factor & Risk Communication**: Education, training, creation of confidence, and stakeholder involvement.

## Risk Communication Goals
- **Education**: Risk awareness and handling.
- **Training**: Inducing behavior change.
- **Confidence**: Developing trust through performance.
- **Involvement**: Participation in decision-making and conflict resolution.
