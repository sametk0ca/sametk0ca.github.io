# Concept: Risk Assessment Methods

## Overview
A range of methods transform vulnerability, threat, likelihood, and impact into a ranked list to prioritize and treat risks.

## Common Methodologies
1. **[[nist|NIST SP800-30/39]]**: US Government's preferred risk assessment methods, strongly regulatory focused.
2. **[[iso-iec|ISO/IEC 27005]]**: International standard for information risk management. Not prescriptive on technique but component-driven focus.
3. **[[stride|STRIDE]]**: Failure-oriented threat modeling approach (Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, Elevation of Privilege).
4. **[[fair|FAIR]]**: Taxonomy of risk factors and a framework for combining them, breaking financial loss factors into multiple levels.
5. **[[octave-allegro|Octave Allegro]]**: Oriented towards operational risk and security practices rather than technology.
6. **[[attack-trees|Attack Trees]]**: Root node is the attacker's goal, sub-nodes are actions leading to successful compromise.
7. **IRAM 2**: Information Security Forum (ISF) methodology using identification, evaluation, and treatment phases.

## Component-driven vs. System-driven
- **Component-driven (Bottom-up)**: Focus on individual technical components (hardware, software, data, staff).
- **System-driven (Top-down)**: Analyze systems as a whole, focusing on goals and interactions between parts.
