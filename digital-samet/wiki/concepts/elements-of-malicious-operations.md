# Concept: Elements of a Malicious Operation

## 1. Infection Vectors
- **Malicious Attachments**: Delivering malware via email using social engineering.
- **Black Hat SEO**: Manipulating search engine rankings to lead victims to malicious sites.
- **Drive-by Downloads**: Automatically installing malware when a victim visits a compromised or malicious page, often using **Exploit Kits**.
- **Malvertising**: Injecting malicious scripts into legitimate advertisement networks.

## 2. Technical Infrastructure
- **Command & Control (C2)**: Redundant systems (Multi-tier, P2P, Fast Flux) used to manage botnets and evade takedowns.
- **Bulletproof Hosting**: Providers that ignore abuse complaints and legal requests, often by operating in lax jurisdictions.

## 3. Specialized Criminal Services
- **Exploit Kits**: Web-based tools that collect multiple vulnerabilities to automate the infection of visitors.
- **Pay-Per-Install (PPI)**: Services where one criminal pays another to install their malware on an already infected pool of computers.
- **CAPTCHA Solving**: Crowdsourced or automated services to bypass bot protections.

## 4. Human & Financial Elements
- **Money Mules**: Individuals recruited to launder stolen funds, moving money from traceable to untraceable channels.
- **Reshipping Mules**: Unsuspecting individuals used to receive and forward goods purchased with stolen credit cards.
- **Fake Accounts**: Large-scale creation and sale of social media or email accounts, often with "reputation boosting" services (fake likes/followers).

## 5. Payment Methods
- **Credit Card Processors**: Preferred by criminals for reach, requiring high-fee "high risk" banks and customer support to minimize chargebacks.
- **Cryptocurrencies**: The current standard for anonymity (e.g., Bitcoin, Monero), though transactions can still be traced and exchanges are risky.
- **Untraceable Methods**: Western Union, MoneyGram, and historical virtual currencies like Liberty Reserve.
