# Concept: Trust Services and Electronic Signatures

## Overview
Trust services provide assurance for electronic transactions, typically using Public Key Infrastructure (PKI). Legal frameworks (like **eIDAS** in the EU) aim to give electronic signatures legal equivalence to wet-ink signatures.

## Key Legal Issues
1. **Admissibility**: Electronic data is now widely accepted as evidence in legal proceedings, provided its integrity can be forensically demonstrated.
2. **Requirements of Form**: Laws often demand "writing" or "signatures" for specific documents (e.g., wills, property transfers). Trust services bridge this gap.
3. **Liability of Certificate Issuers**:
    - **Negligence**: What duty of care does a CA (Certificate Authority) owe to a relying party?
    - **Liability Limits**: Issuers often try to limit liability through clauses in the certificate itself, though the enforceability of these in consumer contexts is often debated.

## Policy Interventions
States often intervene to support trust services by:
- Mandating legal equivalence for electronic signatures.
- Establishing regulatory frameworks for "qualified" trust service providers.
- Reversing the burden of proof (requiring the issuer to prove they were *not* negligent).
