# Concept: Privacy Law

## Definition
Privacy is often described as the "right to be left alone" or the right to be free from intrusion by others into personal affairs. In cyber security, it most often arises in the context of electronic surveillance and investigatory activity.

## International Norms
- **Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948)**: Article 12 protects against "arbitrary interference" with privacy.
- **European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR)**: Article 8 provides a right to respect for private and family life.
- **US Constitution (Fourth Amendment)**: Protects against "unreasonable searches and seizures".

## Key Aspects
- **Not Absolute**: Privacy is a conditional right, subject to limitations and exceptions (e.g., national security, law enforcement).
- **Metadata vs. Content**: Historically, metadata (data about data) has a lower expectation of privacy, though this is increasingly challenged due to the revealing nature of modern metadata.
- **Intangible Information**: Modern legal norms extend privacy rights to digital/intangible information as well as physical spaces.

## Interception
- **State Interception**: Lawful access for law enforcement or security, often regulated by specific domestic regimes (e.g., warrants).
- **Non-State Interception**: Usually restricted or criminalized (e.g., unauthorized wiretapping or hacking).
