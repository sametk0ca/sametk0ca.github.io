# Concept: Organized Cybercrime Operations

## Overview
Modern cybercrime is rarely a solo act. It is a commoditized ecosystem where specialized actors cooperate to maximize financial gain through various schemes.

## 1. Cyber-Enabled Organized Crime
- **Advance Fee Fraud (419 Scams)**: Luring victims with promises of high rewards in exchange for upfront fees. Includes romance fraud and classified ad scams.
- **Online Drug Trade**: Darknet marketplaces (e.g., Silk Road) using Tor and cryptocurrencies to facilitate anonymous transactions.

## 2. Cyber-Dependent Organized Crime
- **Email Spam**: Mass distribution of unsolicited messages to sell counterfeit goods or deliver malware.
- **Phishing**: Crafting fake login pages to steal user credentials. Facilitated by "phishing kits."
- **Financial Malware**: Stealing banking credentials and credit card info (e.g., Zeus, Torpig).
- **Ransomware**: Encrypting victim files and demanding payment for the decryption key. The "gold standard" for direct monetization.
- **Click Fraud**: Using bots to generate fake ad clicks, defrauding the advertising industry.
- **Cryptojacking**: Unauthorized use of victim hardware (via malware or browser scripts) to mine cryptocurrency.
- **DDoS-for-Hire**: "Stresser" services that allow anyone to purchase bandwidth to knock targets offline.

## 3. The Business Model
- **Affiliate Programs**: Central organizations provide the infrastructure (marketplace, payment processing) while affiliates focus on directing traffic (via spam or malware).
- **Specialization**: Actors specialize in niches like "bulletproof hosting," "exploit kits," or "money mule" recruitment.
