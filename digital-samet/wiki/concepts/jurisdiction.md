# Concept: Jurisdiction

## Definition
Jurisdiction is the practical authority granted to a legal body to administer justice within a defined field of responsibility. In cyber security, it is heavily based on the principle of **territorial sovereignty**.

## Types of Jurisdiction
1. **Prescriptive Jurisdiction**: The authority of a state to make its law applicable to the activities, relations, or status of persons, or the interests of persons in things.
2. **Enforcement Jurisdiction**: The authority of a state to use the resources of government to induce or compel compliance with its law.

## Challenges in Cyberspace
- **Territoriality**: Traditional jurisdiction is based on physical borders, while cyberspace is inherently global.
- **Data Sovereignty**: The concern about which states can compel intervention with data stored on their territory or controlled by persons in their territory.
- **CLOUD Act (US)**: Legislation clarifying that US prescriptive jurisdiction extends to data stored on foreign servers if controlled by a US-based provider.

## International Cooperation
- **Mutual Legal Assistance Treaties (MLATs)**: Formal state-to-state requests for gathering cross-border evidence (often slow).
- **Budapest Convention**: Aims to harmonize cybercrime laws and facilitate international cooperation.
