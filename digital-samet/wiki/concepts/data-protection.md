# Concept: Data Protection

## Definition
Data protection law evolved from privacy law to address modern data processing. In the EU, it is primarily governed by the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**.

## Key Definitions (GDPR)
1. **Data Subject**: An identified or identifiable living natural person.
2. **Personal Data**: Any information relating to a data subject (includes pseudonymized data, IP addresses, etc.).
3. **Processing**: Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, disclosure, erasure).
4. **Controller**: The person or body determining the "purposes and means" of processing.
5. **Processor**: The person or body processing data "on behalf of" the controller.

## Core Obligations
- **Data Security**: Controllers and processors must implement "appropriate technical and organisational measures" to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk.
- **Accountability**: Controllers must be able to demonstrate compliance with data protection principles.
- **Breach Notification**: Requirement to notify authorities (and sometimes data subjects) of significant personal data breaches.

## Global Impact
- **Adequacy Determinations**: Allows transfer of personal data outside the EU if the third country ensures an adequate level of protection.
- **Extra-territorial Scope**: GDPR claims prescriptive jurisdiction over personal data processing anywhere that relates to data subjects present in the EU.
