# Concept: Risk

## Definition
According to [[ortwin-renn|Ortwin Renn]], risk is the "possibility that human actions or events lead to consequences that have an impact on what humans value."

## Key Elements of Risk
1. **[[vulnerability|Vulnerability]]**: Attributes of a system that can be targeted (e.g., susceptibility to deception, hardware flaws, software exploits).
2. **[[threat|Threat]]**: An individual, event, or action that has the capability to exploit a vulnerability.
3. **[[likelihood|Likelihood]]**: Degree of possibility that a threat will exploit a vulnerability.
4. **[[impact|Impact]]**: Negative result of a threat exploiting a vulnerability on the success of the system objectives.

## Outcomes of Risk
- **Intolerable**: Risk must be abandoned or replaced.
- **Tolerable**: Risk reduced with reasonable and appropriate methods (ALARP/ALARA).
- **Acceptable**: Risk reduction not necessary.

## Types of Risk
- **Routine**: Follow normal decision-making process.
- **Complex**: Requires broader evidence and comparative approach (e.g., cost-benefit analysis).
- **Uncertain**: Lack of predictability, requires a precautionary approach and resilience.
- **Ambiguous**: Different interpretations from stakeholders, requires participatory decision-making.
