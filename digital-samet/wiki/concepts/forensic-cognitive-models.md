# Forensic Cognitive Models

- **Source:** [[ka-forensics]]
- **Tags:** #forensics #cognition #analysis

## Overview
Forensic cognitive models describe how experts perform investigations, moving from raw data to presentable results. The most prominent model adapted for forensics is the Pirolli & Card sense-making loop.

## Cognitive Task Model (Pirolli & Card)
This model (originally for intelligence analysis) describes the transformation of information along two main loops:

### 1. The Foraging Loop
A balancing act between three types of processing to manage the "mountain" of raw data:
- **Explore:** Expanding the scope of potential evidence ("the shoebox").
- **Enrich:** Narrowing down data with specific queries/filters.
- **Exploit:** Reading and analyzing artifacts to extract facts.

### 2. The Sense-making Loop
The ability to make sense of an ambiguous situation and understanding connections between people, places, and events:
- **Problem Structuring:** Creating and exploring hypotheses.
- **Evidentiary Reasoning:** Using evidence to support or disprove hypotheses.
- **Decision Making:** Selecting a course of action from alternatives.

## Analytical Processes

### Bottom-up Processes (Data-Driven)
Building higher-level representations from specific evidence:
1. **Search and Filter:** Eliminating irrelevant data.
2. **Read and Extract:** Extracting facts and facts relationships.
3. **Schematize:** Organizing facts (e.g., timelines, graphs).
4. **Build Case:** Developing testable theories or working hypotheses.
5. **Tell Story:** Final presentation and reporting.

### Top-down Processes (Theory-Driven)
Providing context and direction for analysis:
- **Re-evaluate:** Feedback or new theories requiring re-examination.
- **Search for Support/Evidence/Relations:** Seeking specific data to validate a hypothesis.
