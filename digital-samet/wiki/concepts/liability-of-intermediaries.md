# Concept: Liability of Intermediaries

## Overview
Internet intermediaries (ISPs, host providers, search engines) are often shielded from liability for content created by their users under specific "safe harbor" or "shield" laws.

## Liability Shields (EU Ecommerce Directive)
The EU provides three main categories of shields:
1. **Mere Conduit**: ISPs just transmitting data. Widely exempted from liability.
2. **Caching**: Automatic, intermediate, and temporary storage of data.
3. **Hosting**: Storing data provided by a user (e.g., cloud storage, social media).

## "Notice and Take-down"
- **Knowledge Requirement**: Hosting providers are usually exempt until they have "reason to know" of illicit content.
- **Expeditious Removal**: Once notified, they must act quickly to remove or disable access to the offending content to maintain their liability shield.

## US Context
- **DMCA Section 512**: Provides a similar "safe harbor" for copyright infringement.
- **Section 230 (Communications Decency Act)**: Broadly shields platforms from liability for most user-generated content (with some recent exceptions like sex trafficking).
