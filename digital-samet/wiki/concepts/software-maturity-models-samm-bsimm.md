# Software Security Maturity Models (SAMM and BSIMM)

- **Source:** [[ka-secure-software-lifecycle]]
- **Tags:** #sdlc #maturity-models #governance #samm #bsimm

## Overview
Maturity models help organizations evaluate their current security posture, define goals, and measure progress in their secure software lifecycle.

## 1. OWASP SAMM (Software Assurance Maturity Model)
A **prescriptive** framework that tells organizations what they *should* do to improve security.
- **Structure:** 12 security practices grouped into 4 business functions (Governance, Construction, Verification, Deployment).
- **Maturity Levels:**
    - **0:** Activity unfulfilled.
    - **1:** Initial understanding / ad hoc.
    - **2:** Increasing efficiency / effectiveness.
    - **3:** Comprehensive mastery at scale.
- **Use Case:** Formulating a tailored strategy and building a roadmap for improvement.

## 2. BSIMM (Building Security In Maturity Model)
A **descriptive** model that reflects what organizations are *actually* doing in the real world.
- **Data-driven:** Based on in-person interviews and measurements from hundreds of companies (e.g., Microsoft, Google, banks).
- **Structure:** 12 practices grouped into 4 domains (Governance, Intelligence, SSDL Touchpoints, Deployment).
- **Use Case:** Benchmarking an organization's activities against industry peers.

## 3. Key Difference
- **SAMM is normative:** "Do this to be secure."
- **BSIMM is observational:** "Here is what others are doing."
