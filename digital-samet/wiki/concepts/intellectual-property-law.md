# Concept: Intellectual Property Law

## Overview
Intellectual Property (IP) rights are "negative rights" that allow owners to prohibit others from certain activities. They intersect with cyber security in areas like software protection, reverse engineering, and trade secrets.

## Key IP Rights
1. **Copyright**:
    - Unregistered right that protects "literary works" (including software source and executable code).
    - Protects the **expression** of an idea, not the idea/functionality itself.
    - Term: Usually life of author + 70 years.
2. **Patents**:
    - Registered right for novel, non-obvious inventions.
    - Protects **functionality** and inventive steps.
    - Software-enabled inventions (including cryptographic methods) can be patentable.
    - Term: Usually 20 years from application.
3. **Trademarks**:
    - Registered signs/symbols used to distinguish products/services.
    - Relevant in domain name disputes and "certification marks" (e.g., security standards compliance).
4. **Trade Secrets**:
    - Protects confidential, valuable information (e.g., algorithms, customer lists).
    - Requires "reasonable efforts" to maintain secrecy.
    - Protected as long as they remain secret.

## Reverse Engineering
- Traditionally seen as "fair game" for trade secrets (extracting know-how from a public artifact).
- **Legal Shifts**: Modern laws (like the DMCA in the US) prohibit circumvention of **Technological Protection Measures (TPM)**, making reverse engineering for security research complex and potentially risky.
- **EU Law**: Generally permits observing/studying software by an authorized user and limited reverse compilation for interoperability.

## Remedies
- **Civil**: Monetary damages, injunctions (orders to cease activity), or "take-down" orders.
- **Criminal**: For egregious copyright/trademark infringement or theft of trade secrets (e.g., industrial espionage).
