# Concept: State Actors and Hacktivists

## 1. State Actors (Nation States)
Driven by national interest, these actors have virtually unlimited resources and often target specific high-value objectives.

### Key Activities
- **Sabotage**: Disrupting critical infrastructure. **Stuxnet** is the archetypal example, targeting nuclear enrichment facilities.
- **Espionage**: Long-term spying on governments, corporations, and activists. Often categorized as **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)**.
- **Disinformation**: Spreading false information or polarizing online discussions through troll accounts and botnets.

### Differences from Commodity Crime
- **Targeting**: State attacks are highly tailored to specific victims, whereas commodity crime is broad.
- **Persistence**: State actors can wait years to finalize an attack; criminals need fast ROI.
- **Sophistication**: Frequent use of **Zero-day exploits** and custom-built tools.

## 2. Hacktivists
Driven by political or social goals rather than profit.

### Common Tactics
- **Netstrikes & DDoS**: Overwhelming websites of corporations or governments as a form of protest.
- **Data Leaks**: Stealing and releasing sensitive documents to the public (e.g., Wikileaks, Anonymous).
- **Web Defacements**: Changing a target website's homepage to display a political message. Often used by early-career attackers to "prove their worth."

## 3. Insider Threats
Disgruntled employees or contractors who use their authorized access to perform sabotage or theft (e.g., Maroochy Water Services incident).
