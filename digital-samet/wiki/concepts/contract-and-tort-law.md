# Concept: Contract and Tort Law

## 1. Contract Law
A **contract** is a volitional legal relationship between two or more persons, often defined as "a promise that the law will enforce."

### Key Elements of Online Contracts
- **Sufficiency of Communication**: Offer and Acceptance.
- **Contractual Trigger**: The precise moment a contract is formed. In online transactions, this is typically when the sender of the offer receives the acceptance.
- **Electronic Commerce Directive (EU)**: Orders and acknowledgments are deemed received when they become accessible to the receiving party (e.g., server logs).

### Security via Contract
Contracts are a primary mechanism for enforcing security standards:
- **Supply Chain**: Procurement agreements often mandate compliance with standards like ISO 27001 or PCI DSS.
- **Closed Systems**: Membership contracts in trading or payment platforms (e.g., SWIFT, Visa/Mastercard) enforce strict security and authentication protocols.

## 2. Tort Law
A **tort** is a civil wrong other than a breach of contract. Unlike contracts, tort liability does not require a prior relationship between the parties.

### Negligence
The most common tort in cyber security. It involves a failure to fulfill a **duty of care**, causing harm to a victim.
- **Duty of Care**: Exists if the parties are proximate, harm is "reasonably foreseeable," and it is fair to impose responsibility.
- **Foreseeability**: Measured by what an objectively reasonable person would have foreseen. Failure to plan or "failure of imagination" is not an excuse.

### Cyber Security Duty of Care Examples
| Potential Tortfeasor | Activity | Potential Victims |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Retail Merchant** | Maintaining payment card security | Card holders, Banks |
| **Email Provider** | Managing email server security | Subscribers, their correspondents |
| **Business Enterprise** | Managing IT/OT security | Staff, Supply chain partners, Third parties harmed by pivot attacks |
| **Software Developer** | Implementing crypto protocols | Users, Merchants, SaaS providers |

## 3. Key Differences
- **Contract**: Based on agreement/promises. Remedies usually require proving financial harm.
- **Tort**: Based on social duties/obligations. Does not require a prior agreement.
