# Concept: Introductory Principles of Law

## Law vs. Science
- **Science**: Discovery of immutable principles (e.g., physics, chemistry).
- **Law**: Social and political values, human desire, and human frailty. It changes as society evolves.

## Primary Legal Systems
1. **[[common-law|Common Law]]**: Predominant in anglophone territories (UK, US, etc.). Relies heavily on judicial precedents (case law).
2. **[[civil-law|Civil Law]]**: Based on codified statutes (comprehensive legal codes). Predominant in most of Europe and Latin America.

## Sources of Law
- **Primary Legislation**: Statutes enacted by a legislative body (e.g., Acts of Parliament).
- **Secondary Legislation**: Law-making authority delegated to agencies or departments.
- **Case Law (Judicial Precedent)**: Decisions made by courts that interpret laws.

## Substantive vs. Procedural Law
- **[[substantive-law|Substantive Law]]**: Focuses on obligations, responsibilities, and behaviors (e.g., computer crime, contract, tort, data protection).
- **[[procedural-law|Procedural Law]]**: Focuses on the dispute resolution process (e.g., civil procedure, criminal procedure, rules of evidence).
