# Source: KA Human Factors

## Summary
This Knowledge Area (KA) explores the foundational role of human behavior in cyber security. It emphasizes that security measures cannot be effective if humans are neither willing nor able to use them. The core message is to "fit the task to the human" rather than the other way around.

## Key Themes
- **Usable Security**: Security must be effective, efficient, and satisfying for the user.
- **Psychological Acceptability**: Security mechanisms must be acceptable to those who apply them (Saltzer & Schroeder).
- **Human Error**: Errors are often caused by poorly designed systems or unsustainable workloads ("rule-bending" for productivity).
- **Cognitive Limitations**: Humans have finite attention and memory (STM vs LTM).
- **Mental Models**: Users' internal understandings of how systems work influence their security decisions.
- **Security Culture**: Moving from "Fear, Uncertainty, and Doubt" (FUD) to a positive, supportive security environment.
